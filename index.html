Evaluation Methodology

Scoring Rubric
  • Clarity — Is the response well-structured and easy to understand?
  • Factual Alignment — Does output match verifiable facts or source material?
  • Completeness — Does the response fully address the prompt?
  • Logical Consistency — Is the reasoning coherent and free of contradictions?
Issue Taxonomy
  • Hallucination — Fabricated facts or citations not grounded in reality
  • Vague Reasoning — Non-specific or circular logic that avoids the core question
  • Ambiguous Output — Multiple valid interpretations; unclear intent
  • Overconfidence — High-certainty claims without sufficient grounding
Process
  • Each prompt evaluated independently across all 4 criteria
  • Scores assigned on a 1–10 scale; overall = arithmetic mean
  • Flags are non-exclusive — multiple issues can coexist
  • Notes document iterative refinements to criteria definitions
